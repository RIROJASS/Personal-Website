Pampa La Cruz Archaeological Expedition

4-Week intensive archaeological field program with the University of Florida at Huanchaco, Peru, working on the Pampa La Cruz site under the supervision of Dr. Gabriel Prieto.

Field Work Highlights:

  • Participated in excavations of a Chim√∫ child sacrifice site dating to the 15th century
  • Created drone orthophotography maps of the excavation site at different stages
  • Developed 3D site models using Agisoft Metascape and AutoCAD
  • Studied osteological remains under the guidance of Dr. John Verano
  • Cataloged artifacts including ceramics, textiles, and ritual items

This field experience was transformative, providing hands-on archaeological training while introducing me to the vibrant coastal culture of Peru. Beyond the academic work, I embraced local experiences including surfing the famous waves of Huanchaco and enjoying fresh ceviche after long days in the field.

AI-Enhanced Remote Sensing for Archaeological Discovery

Ongoing research applying machine learning algorithms to satellite imagery for the identification of archaeological sites, with a focus on areas that are difficult to access or environmentally sensitive.

Research Objectives:

  • Develop neural networks trained to recognize patterns associated with archaeological features
  • Analyze multispectral and LiDAR data to detect subsurface anomalies
  • Create predictive models for identifying high-probability areas for archaeological investigation
  • Establish protocols for non-invasive site assessment using AI-enhanced remote sensing

This research bridges the gap between cutting-edge AI techniques and archaeological methods, offering new pathways for discovery while minimizing physical disturbance to potential sites.

I will be presenting this research at the 2025 Florida Undergraduate Research Conference, highlighting how these methods have already helped identify previously unknown archaeological features in test regions.
