Research & Field Work

My intellectual journey across the world, physically and digitally

Florida Undergraduate Research Conference 2025

I presented my research on "Finding Lost Archaeological Sites with AI: Object Detection with Remote Sensing" at the 2025 Florida Undergraduate Research Conference alongside lab partner Olivia Zhang.

Presentation Focus:

  • Demonstrating how machine learning algorithms can identify potential archaeological sites from satellite imagery
  • Discussing the ethical implications and benefits of non-invasive archaeological techniques
  • Showcasing the accuracy improvements over traditional methods
  • Explaining the pros and cons to such methods, associated caveats, and pit-falls to avoid
  • Exploring future applications and directions for this technology

AI-Enhanced Remote Sensing for Archaeological Discovery

Ongoing research applying machine learning algorithms to satellite imagery for the identification of archaeological sites, with a focus on raised field structures in the Llanos de Moxos area in Beni, Colombia.

Research Objectives:

  • Develop neural networks trained to recognize patterns associated with raised field features
  • Create predictive models for identifying high-probability areas for archaeological investigation
  • Establish protocols for non-invasive site assessment using AI-enhanced remote sensing

This research bridges the gap between cutting-edge AI techniques and archaeological methods, offering new pathways for discovery while minimizing physical disturbance to potential sites.
